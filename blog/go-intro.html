Mapcontrol Go: Charting New Paths to Solutions that Matter

10-04-2020 07:00 UTC

POSTED BY LEO KLENNER, HENRY FUNG, AND JJ LEE

As early career professionals, we’ve seen first-hand how critical it is to define your role by taking on open-ended problems. We started Mapcontrol, because too few early career professionals have the right training for solving open-ended problems and too few organizations know how to provide it.

At Mapcontrol, we’re building Go, the first growth program for decomposition strategy. Go helps early career professionals take on open-ended problems and define their roles.

Define Your Role

In more and more organizations today, it is up to you to define the role you’ve been hired for and pathfind your way toward an impactful career. But to do this, you need a skillset that is often very different from the one that got you hired.

Defining your role is hard, because you need to uncover and solve impactful problems. The most impactful problems will often be ambiguous and open-ended. Your organization may only have a hunch about these problems. So without much feedback or outside guidance you need to evaluate why a chosen problem matters, how to scope it and how to build a solution.

Solve Hard Problems

Decomposition strategy is the process of breaking down open-ended problems into smaller components and reassembling them into a solution to drive business value.

At the core of this strategy are decomposition loops, a heuristic we developed to deliver concrete solutions to real-world open-ended problems. Decomposition loops structure the process of breaking down large decision spaces based on the continuous construction and testing of assumptions. Go centers on building and deploying decomposition loops against ambiguous environments to create impactful solutions.

Go is currently in development and being tested at Johns Hopkins University.
