Chart New Paths to Solutions that Matter

Mapcontrol is building Go, a growth program that empowers early career professionals to take on open-ended problems and achieve meaningful impact.

Go is built on courseware that our team created over two years at Johns Hopkins University. At Hopkins, we created pioneering professional development courses on problem decomposition and strategy, helping early career professionals chart new paths to solutions that matter.

The Problem We Solve

Organizations increasingly need employees to define their roles for themselves by taking on open-ended problems. But too few early career professionals have the right training to tackle open-ended problems. As a result, early career professionals often lack the skillset needed to take ownership of their roles and achieve meaningful impact.

At Mapcontrol, we’re building Go, a growth program that trains early career professionals to take on open-ended problems and chart new paths for their roles. Go trains Decomposition Strategy, the process of breaking down open-ended problems into smaller components, reassembling them into a solution, and driving value with the solution.

Our Team

Go is developed by Leo Klenner, Henry Fung and JJ Lee. Our team has researched counterinsurgency, sold enterprise software, built training simulations, applied algorithms to global development, and worked on regulatory policy for new technologies. Go is built on the skills that have allowed us to succeed across these diverse experiences.
